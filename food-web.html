🤔 What If?

Use your food web knowledge to predict what happens!

⚠️ Scenario 1: Frogs disappear!
All the frogs in this ecosystem suddenly disappear from a disease. What would MOST LIKELY happen to the grasshopper population?
🐸 Frogs eat grasshoppers. Remove frogs → grasshoppers face less predation → grasshopper population INCREASES. This cascade effect shows how removing one organism affects the whole web!
⚠️ Scenario 2: Grass is destroyed!
A drought kills most of the grass in the ecosystem. Which organism would be MOST DIRECTLY affected first?
🌿 Grasshoppers and rabbits are PRIMARY CONSUMERS — they eat grass directly. Remove the grass (producer) and they lose their food source immediately. Effects then cascade UP the food web to frogs, snakes, and hawks.
⚠️ Scenario 3: Who's the decomposer?
Which organism in this food web is a DECOMPOSER, and what is its role?
🍄 The mushroom is a DECOMPOSER. Decomposers (fungi, bacteria) break down dead organisms and return nutrients to the soil. Without decomposers, dead matter would pile up and nutrients would never be recycled back to producers like grass!
