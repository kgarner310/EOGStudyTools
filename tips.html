🎯 Test-Taking Strategies
1
Read the WHOLE question before looking at the answer choices. The last few words often change everything!
2
Eliminate wrong answers first. Cross out the 2 obviously wrong ones, then choose between the remaining 2. You just went from 25% to 50%!
3
Spot the key science words: physical/chemical, inherited/acquired, abiotic/biotic, balanced/unbalanced. They tell you exactly what the question is about!
4
Item sets (passage + questions): Read the passage carefully — the answer is ALWAYS supported by information in the passage or your science knowledge. Don't overthink it!
5
Watch for "NOT" and "EXCEPT"! These flip the question. Slow down and re-read any question with these words.
6
Skip hard questions and come back. Don't get stuck! Mark it, move on, and return with fresh eyes. Never leave a blank — guess if you have to!
7
For "Most likely" food web questions — trace the energy flow. Removing a predator = its prey INCREASES. Removing a prey = its predator DECREASES.
8
Don't change your first answer unless you're absolutely sure. Your gut instinct is usually right!
🔬 Domain Quick Reference
⚗️ Physical Science — Forces, Matter & Changes ▼ tap to expand
  • Physical change: same substance, different form — ice melting, tearing paper, dissolving salt
  • Chemical change: NEW substance formed — burning, rusting, souring, cooking. Signs: light, heat, gas, color change
  • Physical property: observed without changing the substance — color, density, solubility, boiling point, magnetism
  • Chemical property: describes how it reacts — flammability, reactivity, ability to rust or tarnish
  • Balanced forces: net force = 0 → NO change in motion (stays still OR keeps same speed)
  • Unbalanced forces: net force ≠ 0 → motion CHANGES (speeds up, slows down, changes direction)
  • Non-contact forces: gravity, magnetism — act at a distance WITHOUT touching
  • Contact forces: friction (opposes motion), applied force (push/pull) — require touching
  • Mixture: physically combined, can be separated — saltwater, trail mix, air
  • Pure substance: fixed composition — water (H₂O), gold (Au), oxygen (O₂)
  • To separate mixtures: use physical properties — magnet for iron, filter for size, evaporation for solutions
🌿 Life Science — Cells, Ecosystems & Heredity ▼ tap to expand
  • Mitochondria: energy (ATP) — "powerhouse" — in BOTH plant and animal cells
  • Chloroplast: photosynthesis — in PLANT cells ONLY — captures sunlight to make food
  • Cell membrane: gatekeeper — controls what enters and exits — in ALL cells
  • Nucleus: DNA control center — directs cell activities
  • Circulatory system: heart + blood vessels → delivers oxygen to cells
  • Respiratory system: lungs → gas exchange (O₂ in, CO₂ out)
  • Digestive system: breaks food into nutrients the body uses
  • Food web order: Sun → Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer → Apex Predator
  • Decomposers: bacteria and fungi — break down dead matter, return nutrients to soil
  • Abiotic factors: NON-living — sunlight, water, temperature, soil, air
  • Biotic factors: LIVING organisms — plants, animals, bacteria, fungi
  • Inherited trait: from DNA (parents) — eye color, blood type, height potential
  • Acquired trait: from experience — speaking a language, playing softball, scars
  • Natural selection: better-adapted traits → survive → reproduce → passed on → population changes
🌍 Earth Science — Rocks, Water Cycle & Earth's Layers ▼ tap to expand
  • Evaporation: liquid → gas (powered by the SUN)
  • Condensation: gas → liquid (forms clouds)
  • Precipitation: water falls from clouds (rain, snow, sleet, hail)
  • Transpiration: plants release water vapor through leaves
  • Main driver of water cycle: THE SUN (provides heat energy for evaporation)
  • Igneous rock: cooled lava or magma — examples: basalt, granite
  • Sedimentary rock: compressed layers — FOSSILS ARE FOUND HERE — limestone, sandstone
  • Metamorphic rock: heat + pressure transforms existing rock — marble, slate, quartzite
  • Weathering: BREAKING rocks into smaller pieces (wind, water, ice, acid)
  • Erosion: MOVING broken rock pieces to new locations
  • Earth layers: Crust (thinnest) → Mantle (thickest) → Outer Core (liquid iron = magnetic field) → Inner Core (solid)
  • Climate vs weather: Climate = 30+ year average. Weather = today's conditions
